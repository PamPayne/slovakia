## Licenses

The operator will require a license to operate in Slovakia.

There are two types of licenses:

a. general licence – Non-cash lotteries, raffles and Non-Casino Card Games may be operated under a general licence (N/A for QF)

b. specific license – (see below)

Licences may only be granted or issued to legal entities established in the Slovak Republic or in another Member State.

Licences may only be granted or issued to foreign invested enterprises with their registered office or permanent address in another Member State

Individual Licences may be granted for a maximum of five years for operating online games in Online Casinos.

Share capital - Applicants must have a minimum equity of EUR 1,700,000 for games of chance in Online Casinos. The share capital must be paid up by no later than the date of the application for an individual License.

## Responsible Gaming & Self Restriction Requirements

Online Gambling Operator shall be required to ensure that players can register settings for self-restricting measures.

Online Gambling Operators shall not enable players who have registered a self-restricting measure to place bets above the limit specified in the self-restricting measure after it has been set.

(E.g. if a player has registered settings for a loss limit, the online Gambling Operator shall be required to ensure that the Player cannot continue playing the online game of chance from when the set loss limit has been exceeded).

Comment: The operator must provide the player with the facility to set limits (i.e. loss, wager, bet, deposit as examples). As per point (g) in the Player protection section below – it appears the loss and bet limits are compulsory limits that should be offered to the player.

## Player Protection Content

Whenever players log in to their player accounts, online Gambling Operators shall be required to provide access to the following information, in a visible location, for the entire time that the players play the respective games of chance:

- (a) the following statement: "Zákaz hrania hazardných hier osobám mladším ako 18 rokov." ["Gambling is prohibited for persons under the age of 18."];
- (b) information on the health risks associated with excessive gambling;
- (c) the following warning: "Hazardné hry predstavujú riziko vysokých finančných strát." ["Gambling can cause significant financial losses."];
- (d) information on the player's total bets and winnings over the time played on that player account, cumulatively for the calendar month, and for his/her overall time spent playing on that player account, cumulatively since the player account in question was set up;
- (e) the telephone number or hyperlink to the website of a medical facility specialising in prevention, diagnosis and treatment of substance and non-substance addictions, published by the Authority on its website;
- (f) a hypertext link to the Register Administrator's website;
- (g) information on the possibility of setting self-restricting measures, specifically a maximum for total bets and a maximum for the player's losses over the time played on that Player account.

Online Gambling Operators shall be required to ensure that the information as specified above is displayed for at least five seconds whenever a player logs in to his/her player account.

If the above information displayed for players, in a visible location at any time during a game of chance, the said information must only stop being displayed following active confirmation from the player that he/she wishes to stop it being displayed.

## Games

Games which do not guarantee equal conditions, including the chances of winning, to all players, in particular pyramid games and chain games, are prohibited.

When monitoring data for various games of chance, it is important to ensure identification of each individual game of chance in a way that ensures clear allocation of bets and winnings to the specific game of chance

### Chapter 25 - Conditions applicable to Operation of Games of Chance on slot machines

Quickspin disabled - The time between the start and end of one game of chance must be at least four seconds

RTP - The pay-out ratio must not be lower than 70 %.

Max Bet Limit and Win Limit - The maximum bet shall be ten eurocents per game of chance and the maximum winnings in one game of chance must not exceed five euros; this shall not apply for slot machines operated in Casinos.

The interval between payment of winnings and the start of the next game of chance must be at least 15 seconds; this shall not apply for slot machines operated in Casinos;

Comment: The assumption/interpretation of the above requirements for Chapter 25, is that these requirements are applicable to Landbased slot machines. We would need to confirm if the RTP rate for online casinos is the same and if the four second rule applies to online casinos as well.

The max bet limit, max win limit and interval requirement I assume will apply to landbased areas such as gambling halls and not online.

### Section 26 - Conditions applicable to Operation of Games of Chance on video gaming terminals

RTP - The pay-out ratio must not be lower than 70 %.

Max Bet - The maximum bet in one game of chance must not exceed EUR 10. This restriction shall not apply to operation of video gaming terminals in Casinos.

Comment: The assumption/interpretation of the above requirements for Chapter 26, is that they are applicable to Landbased (physical video gaming terminals) which are similar to slot machines.

We would need to confirm if the RTP rate for online casinos is the same.

The max bet limit, max win limit and interval requirement I assume will apply to landbased areas such as gambling halls and not online.

## Domain

Online Gambling Operators operating online games of chance shall only be entitled to operate online games of chance where their website address ends with the suffix "sk”

## Certification

Full certification of the Gaming System will be required to be completed before an Operator can go live. Games must be certified.

Legal entities with their registered office in the Slovak Republic, which are authorized for assessment shall be authorized to act as accredited testing facilities.

Legal entities with their registered office or a branch in another Member State, which have been appointed by another Member State to perform conformity assessment and which are registered in the list published on the European Commission website, shall also be authorized to act as accredited testing facilities.

## External Register

External register checks are required. The Register of Excluded Individuals is a public administration information system which records all individuals barred from taking part in the games of chance.
